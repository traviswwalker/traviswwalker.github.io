Facilities

The Walker Research Group has laboratories that are located on the first and second floors of the Chemical and Biological Engineering / Chemistry Building (CBEC) in rooms 132 and 201 on the campus of South Dakota School of Mines & Technology in Rapid City, South Dakota. The laboratory spaces include four fume hoods, multiple optical tables, and several pieces of specialized equipment. Information about the various pieces of equipment available in our laboratories is listed below.

SDM will join SD INBRE (NIH IDeA Networks of Biomedical Research Excellence), the state-wide network with NIH NIGMS funding since 2001, as a partner in 2025.09. As a part of this venture, the SD INBRE Research Match Core (RMC), a collection of research laboratories at USD and BHSU, is adding a fourth site, the new Tissue Engineering and Cell Culture Laboratory (TECCL), which is being actively built in Vanderboom Laboratory for Entrepreneurial Research (aka V-Lab) in room 166. This facility, approximately 2,000 sq. ft. in size, will house both TECCL and the accompanying Soft Matter (SoMa) Characterization Facility. The alteration and renovation costs for TECCL are being provided by the impending NIH INBRE award. The SoMa facilities are being built with various contributions. Equipment is being provided by multiple groups from across SDM, as well as donations from off-campus entities that have already been secured. Items from the Walker Research Group that are moving to TECCL/SoMa are denoted with a dagger symbol (†)
